What the modern SERP actually is

The Search Engine Results Page (SERP) is no longer “10 blue links”. It is a dynamic canvas where organic listings, sponsored results, rich snippets, AI-generated answers and local modules compete for attention.

From the blog · Decoding the Modern SERP

Use this article as a higher-level companion piece for stakeholders who need the strategic “why” behind this lesson.

For B2B brands, this matters because most research happens long before a demo request. Prospects search for problem-aware and solution-aware queries, comparison pages, and implementation details. If you only track “rankings for one keyword”, you miss the real surface area where your brand can show up.

A simple B2B SERP teardown

In the video, we break down example SERPs for branded, competitor and solution-aware queries. We map which elements are controllable via content, schema and third-party platforms, and which are outside our control.

Once you’re happy with the snippet, save it as the new control for future experiments.

Connecting SERP visibility to funnel metrics

A 1–2 point increase in organic CTR might sound small. But once you apply it to your full B2B funnel, it can translate into dozens of extra opportunities and meaningful pipeline lift.
